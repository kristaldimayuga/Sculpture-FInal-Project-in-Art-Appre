Classical Sculptures

The Pietà

Artist: Michelangelo
Location: St. Peter's Basilica, Vatican City
Description: This Renaissance sculpture depicts the Virgin Mary holding the dead body of Jesus Christ after his crucifixion, capturing sorrow and piety.

The Great Sphinx of Giza

Artist: Unknown (Attributed to ancient Egyptians of the Old Kingdom)
Location: Giza, Egypt
Description: This colossal limestone statue with a lion's body and a human head represents a guardian figure, embodying strength and wisdom in ancient Egyptian mythology.

The Statue of Liberty

Artist: Frédéric Auguste Bartholdi
Location: Liberty Island, New York Harbor, USA
Description: A gift from France to the United States, this colossal statue symbolizes freedom and democracy, welcoming immigrants arriving by sea.

Modern Sculptures

Cloud Gate (The Bean)

Artist: Anish Kapoor
Location: Millennium Park, Chicago, USA
Description: A reflective, bean-shaped sculpture that invites viewers to engage with the distorted reflections of themselves and the city.

Balloon Dog

Artist: Jeff Koons
Location: Various, including the Broad, Los Angeles, USA
Description: A large, shiny sculpture resembling a twisted balloon animal, exploring themes of childhood and consumerism.

Maman

Artist: Louise Bourgeois
Location: Guggenheim Museum, Bilbao, Spain
Description: A giant spider sculpture symbolizing maternal protection and the complexity of motherhood.

Local Sculptures

Oblation

Artist: Guillermo Tolentino
Location: University of the Philippines Diliman, Quezon City, Philippines
Description: This iconic statue depicts a man with outstretched arms, symbolizing selfless offering and dedication to the country.

People Power Monument

Artist: Eduardo Castrillo
Location: EDSA, Quezon City, Philippines
Description: This monument commemorates the 1986 People Power Revolution, symbolizing the Filipino people's fight for democracy and freedom.

Lapu-Lapu Shrine

Artist: Juan Sajid Imao
Location: Mactan Shrine, Lapu-Lapu City, Cebu, Philippines
Description: This towering bronze statue commemorates Lapu-Lapu, the native chieftain who defeated Ferdinand Magellan in the Battle of Mactan, symbolizing Filipino bravery and resistance against colonization.
